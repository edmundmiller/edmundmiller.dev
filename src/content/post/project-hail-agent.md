My job title says engineer. My PhD trained me as a scientist. I have spent years trying to name the difference between those roles.

_Project Hail Mary_ gave me a useful answer. Rocky makes difficult things work. Ryland Grace figures out what is happening and which experiment could explain it.

AI coding agents make that difference worth examining. They can reduce the cost of implementation. They do not remove the need to choose a question or judge evidence.

## Spoiler warning

This essay discusses major events from _Project Hail Mary_.

## Rocky builds under constraints

Rocky's species builds an interstellar ship on Erid. Rocky keeps that ship working after the rest of his crew dies.

His work becomes more impressive when he meets Grace. Their ships use different materials and support incompatible atmospheres. Rocky still builds a physical connection between them.

He also fabricates parts and repairs equipment during the mission. Every job begins with limits on pressure and heat. Material strength, energy, and time impose more.

Rocky does not solve those problems with a slogan. He measures the system and makes an object that survives its constraints.

That is the engineering skill I recognize. A working result must fit the real system, not only the plan.

Coding agents already help with a narrower version of this loop. They can read documentation, change code, run tests, and inspect a failure. They can repeat those steps without losing patience.

That does not make an agent Rocky. It does explain why implementation is becoming cheaper for many software tasks.

## Grace finds the question

Grace contributes something different. Earth sends him to explain why Astrophage is dimming nearby stars.

He cannot begin with a complete specification. He has to decide which observations matter and which experiment can separate two explanations.

Tau Ceti gives him a useful anomaly. It does not show the same damage as the other nearby stars. Grace treats that exception as evidence instead of noise.

That choice leads the mission toward Taumoeba, an organism that consumes Astrophage. The discovery comes from selecting the right unknown before building the final solution.

Science often starts at that earlier point. The terms are unsettled, the measurements are incomplete, and nobody knows which result will matter.

Engineering and science still overlap. Grace builds experiments, while Rocky makes discoveries. The distinction describes their strongest habits rather than fixed identities.

## What agents make cheaper

An agent can help turn a clear request into tested code. It can trace logs, compare interfaces, and try another implementation after a failed test.

Those abilities matter. They also begin after someone has framed the task.

The harder questions remain:

- Which problem deserves attention?
- What evidence would change the current explanation?
- Which requirement reflects reality rather than habit?
- Who bears the cost when the solution is wrong?

An agent can suggest answers. A person still has to test those answers against the domain and accept responsibility for the result.

This is Richard Hamming's challenge in practical form. He asked researchers to identify the important problems in their field and examine why they were not working on them.

Cheaper implementation weakens one common excuse. It does not tell us which problem is important.

## Keep the whole loop

The useful loop is simple: ask, build, observe, and revise.

Rocky is strongest at building within physical limits. Grace is strongest at finding an explanation from incomplete evidence. The mission needs both skills.

Software work needs both as well. An agent may shorten the build step. The human still has to frame the problem and inspect the result.

The end of the novel makes the point without another metaphor. Grace teaches science on Erid. A civilization of remarkable builders still values someone who can explain why things happen.

That is the job I want agents to create more room for: choosing better questions and learning from what we build.
